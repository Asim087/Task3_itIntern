Nuclear Milestone Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan’s Legacy

On May 28, 1998, under the leadership of Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan, Pakistan successfully conducted its first nuclear test, making it the first Islamic nuclear power. This monumental achievement ensured Pakistan's defense capabilities and marked a proud moment for the nation.

Honoring a National Hero

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan's Prestigious Awards

The Government of Pakistan awarded Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan the Nishan-e-Imtiaz twice for his outstanding contributions to the nation's defense and scientific advancements.

Biography

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan was born on April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, British India. After the partition in 1947, his family migrated to Pakistan. He completed his early education in Karachi and went on to pursue higher studies abroad. He earned a degree in Metallurgical Engineering from the Technical University of Berlin and later obtained a Ph.D. in Metallurgical Engineering from the Catholic University of Leuven in Belgium. Dr. Khan worked in Europe before returning to Pakistan in 1976, where he was entrusted with leading Pakistan’s nuclear program. His efforts were instrumental in making Pakistan a nuclear power, which culminated in the successful nuclear tests in 1998. He passed away on October 10, 2021, leaving behind a legacy as the Father of Pakistan's nuclear program.

End of an Era: Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan's Last Journey

On October 10, 2021, Pakistan lost a national hero, Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan, the father of its nuclear program. His contributions to strengthening the defense of Pakistan will forever be remembered. Dr. Khan's legacy lives on through his remarkable achievements. He was laid to rest in H-8 Graveyard, Islamabad, where people continue to visit his grave to pay tribute to his lifelong dedication to the country
